#🍜 Danny’s Diner — Customer Behavior Analysis (SQL Case Study 1)
📌 Business Problem

Danny owns a small restaurant called Danny’s Diner. He wants to use data to understand:

Which customers spend the most money
How often customers visit the restaurant
What food items are most popular
How membership affects customer behavior
How loyalty points can be improved

👉 The goal is to convert raw sales data into business insights for better decision-making.

🧰 Tools Used
MySQL
SQL (JOIN, GROUP BY, CASE WHEN, Window Functions)
📂 Dataset

The analysis is based on 3 tables:

sales → customer transactions
menu → food items and prices
members → customer membership details
🧾 Solution Summary

Using SQL queries, we analyzed customer behavior step-by-step:

Combined sales with menu using JOINs
Calculated total spend per customer
Identified visit frequency using DISTINCT dates
Found first and last purchases using window functions
Compared behavior before and after membership
Calculated loyalty points using conditional logic
📊 Key Findings
Customer A is the highest spender overall
Customer B is the most frequent visitor
Ramen and sushi are the most popular items
Membership increases customer engagement and spending
Customers change their ordering behavior after joining membership
💡 Business Recommendations
🎯 Focus marketing efforts on high-value customers (A & B)
🎁 Strengthen loyalty program as it increases engagement
🍣 Promote high-performing items like sushi and ramen
📈 Encourage early membership sign-up to increase spending
🧠 Use purchase history for personalized recommendations
